# Integration of ADMET Prediction Tools in Drug Discovery Pipeline

## Early Discovery Phase
- **Virtual screening**: Filter large compound libraries for favorable ADMET properties
- **Hit identification**: Prioritize hits with balanced activity and ADMET profiles
- **Hit-to-lead**: Guide structural modifications to improve ADMET properties
- **Library design**: Incorporate ADMET constraints in combinatorial library design

## Lead Optimization Phase
- **Multi-parameter optimization**: Balance potency with ADMET properties
- **Structure-property relationship**: Identify structural features affecting ADMET
- **Analog design**: Guide medicinal chemistry for ADMET improvement
- **Formulation strategy**: Inform early formulation approaches based on predicted properties

## Preclinical Development
- **Candidate selection**: Compare ADMET profiles across lead candidates
- **Toxicity prediction**: Identify potential safety concerns before in vivo testing
- **Dosing regimen design**: Inform initial PK/PD modeling
- **Species selection**: Guide selection of relevant animal models

## Clinical Development
- **First-in-human dose prediction**: Support initial dose selection
- **Drug-drug interaction risk**: Identify potential metabolic interactions
- **Special population considerations**: Predict PK differences in specific populations
- **Formulation optimization**: Guide formulation adjustments based on human PK

## Regulatory Considerations
- **ICH M7 compliance**: Support computational toxicology assessments
- **Risk assessment**: Provide weight-of-evidence for safety evaluation
- **Impurity qualification**: Assess ADMET properties of impurities and metabolites
- **Post-approval changes**: Evaluate impact of manufacturing changes on bioavailability

## Implementation Strategies
- **Sequential filtering**: Apply increasingly complex models in staged screening
- **Parallel assessment**: Evaluate multiple ADMET endpoints simultaneously
- **Integrated platforms**: Combine ADMET predictions with other drug discovery tools
- **Feedback loops**: Update models with experimental data generated during development
